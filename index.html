Mix and Match

Imagine a 500-pound orange "murder-carpet" in zero-G—essentially a confused, rotating ceiling fan of claws that treats Mission Control like a giant scratching post. Instead of conducting research, this tiger would lose its mind chasing laser guidance systems, accidentally tackling the communication dish and sending the entire crew drifting toward Venus. Forget high-tech sleeping pods; the beast would inevitably choose to sit in a $10 million carbon-fiber crate, proving that if it fits, it orbits. The real danger comes at 3:00 AM during the galactic zoomies, where one powerful kick off the oxygen scrubber could literally knock the space station out of alignment. Between navigating a minefield of drifting, soggy hairballs and terrifying alien scouts who would surely flee at the sight of a striped predator in a jumpsuit, NASA would quickly learn that "one small step for man" is much safer than "one giant leap for a very cranky, floating cat."
