The potato (Solanum tuberosum) is a versatile and nutrient-dense underground tuber, domesticated 7,000–10,000 years ago in the Andean region of South America before becoming a staple food worldwide. As a member of the nightshade family, the potato plant produces edible tubers that serve as a crucial source of carbohydrates, potassium, and vitamin C. With over 5,000 different varieties available, this starchy crop has been selectively bred to thrive in varied climates and is often prepared by baking, boiling, or frying, and is notably the first vegetable to be grown in space.
